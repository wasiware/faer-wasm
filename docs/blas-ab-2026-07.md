## Reading

1. **Level 1/2 parity confirmed** (copy, gemv, ger, trsv): 17 of 20 rows
   are OVERLAP, and no faer win separates anywhere. These operations are
   memory-bound — a shaped loop and faer's path finish in a dead heat
   because both are waiting on the same bytes. The standing policy
   (don't shape what bandwidth already caps) holds.
2. **The surprise is Level 3.** The working number for R — gemm
   throughput over streaming-loop throughput, the quantity the whole
   shaping strategy is calibrated against — has been ~2 on the runner.
   On THIS machine, measured directly, the flat SIMD column-axpy gemm
   comes within 4–10% of faer's blocked gemm at every size (loop/faer
   0.90–0.96, never separated). syrk reads at loop-parity-or-better
   (1.00–1.17, never separated; the triangular-aware loop skips half
   the writes). Only trsm (0.83–0.87 at n≥512) and trmm at 1024 (0.94)
   produce separated faer wins.
3. **Policy consequence: none yet — the verdicts do not justify
   replacing anything.** faer's paths never lose separated; the loop's
   apparent edges never separate. The unchanged rows stay unchanged.
4. **Thesis consequence if the runner replicates this: R is
   machine-dependent and possibly much closer to 1 than 2.** That would
   sharpen, not weaken, the common-thread story: (a) the case for
   deleting mid-layer blocked machinery gets stronger (even less gemm
   advantage to buy back a surcharge σ); (b) faer's 5–20× matmul wins
   over Pyodide are then about scipy's OpenBLAS-generic gemm being far
   from the machine ceiling, not about blocking beating streaming;
   (c) the multishift's large-n win over our hand kernel must be
   credited to aggressive early deflation (fewer sweeps — an
   algorithmic effect), not gemm batching. Each of those is checkable.

## Runner replication — three draws (2026-07-18, runs 29631062796 / 29631536777 / 29631542527)

The reference-environment answer, and it inverts the container's L3
reading. Consensus across all three runner draws:

- **n ≤ 512, Level 3: the streaming loop BEATS faer's blocked path on
  every draw, 1.1–1.45×, mostly range-separated.** gemm itself: 1.17×/
  1.21×/1.18×/1.21× (draw 1, all separated), 1.31×/1.07×/1.15×/1.14×
  (draw 2), 1.33×/1.18×/1.17×/1.22× (draw 3) at n=64–512. syrk, trmm,
  trsm read the same way.
- **n = 1024 is machine-dependent**: draws 1 and 3 stay loop-ahead
  (gemm 1.21×/1.22× separated); draw 2 reads parity-to-faer (gemm
  1.01× OVERLAP, trmm 0.91× faer WINS) — a cache-size effect,
  consistent with the verdict-stability rule.
- Level 2: loop modestly ahead everywhere (1.05–1.25×, frequently
  separated). Level 1 copy: parity.

**Directly measured on the reference machine class, R ≤ 1 through
n = 512.** The container (previous section) was the outlier in
direction — desktop-class hardware is where faer's blocking looks
best, and even there it only reached parity.

## Standing consequences (recorded, not acted on — tuning freeze)

1. The kernels' "route the O(n³) bulk through faer's gemm" rule leaves
   ~15–20% on the table at L3 shapes ≤ 512 in the reference
   environment. A flat streaming gemm/trmm bulk path for the kernel
   layer is now a measured candidate lever — architect's call, next
   campaign material.
2. The common-thread thesis sharpens to its limit: on the target,
   blocked Level 3 is not merely a small win over streaming — in the
   shipping regime it is not a win at all. The two-speed machine is,
   on the reference class, one speed.
3. faer's 5–20× matmul wins over Pyodide are therefore entirely about
   scipy's OpenBLAS-generic build being far from the machine ceiling.
4. Unchanged rows stay unchanged for now: correctness machinery,
   size budget, and the freeze all say measure twice before rerouting.

Caveat kept open: all A/B builds are the plain simd128 profile; the
relaxed-SIMD (FMA) build could shift the balance toward faer's
microkernels and should be part of any rerouting campaign.

## Step 1 of the BLAS campaign: ceilings + the FMA race (2026-07-18, runs 29634584232/29634587324/29634591008)

Three runner draws, FMA build (`-C target-feature=+simd128,+relaxed-simd`),
three-way race per op: faer vs plain streaming loop vs fused-FMA streaming
loop. Fused numerics cross-checked against faer before timing.

**Ceilings (the roofline the campaign now scores against):**
- memory bandwidth (triad, 24 MB): 12.2–17.8 GB/s across draws
  — **SUPERSEDED (step 3): the probe allocated inside the timed
  region; the fixed probe reads ~32 GB/s on the same runner class**
- peak f64 SIMD arithmetic: ~12.5 GFLOP/s plain build, **23.1–25.3
  GFLOP/s FMA build** (fusing doubles the machine's ceiling, as theory
  says)
- the streaming gemm runs at ~6.2–6.8 GFLOP/s = **~55% of the plain
  ceiling, 27% of the FMA ceiling** — the headroom the build phase
  chases (micro-tiling + per-op FMA)
- `copy` runs at the bandwidth ceiling already — Level 1/2 are done
  before they start

**Verdicts:**
1. **The FMA card does not rescue faer.** With fused instructions on
   both sides available, faer's blocked Level 3 still loses to the
   plain streaming loop on two of three draws at every size (gemm
   loop/faer 1.04–1.35), and splits the third only at n ≥ 512
   (0.94–0.97). The streaming verdict from the plain build stands.
2. **FMA is per-operation, not a blanket win, for our loops:**
   - trmm: fused loop best everywhere, all draws (+10–25% over plain,
     1.11–2.01× over faer)
   - trsm: fused best or tied (+1–13% over plain)
   - gemv: mildly better (+3–36%, small absolute times)
   - gemm: neutral (0.90–1.06 vs plain — at 27% of peak the loop is
     nowhere near compute-bound, so faster arithmetic buys nothing)
   - **syrk: fused is consistently WORSE (−9–26%, all three draws)** —
     the fused instruction's longer latency chain hurts the
     shrinking-column accumulation. A real, replicated negative.
3. Large-n machine-dependence persists (draw 3 leaned faer at
   1024) — the per-op crossover measurement stays in the build phase.

**Design consequence for the BLAS layer:** each operation gets its
variant chosen by measurement — fused for trmm/trsm/gemv, plain for
gemm/syrk — with the FMA build detected at compile time and both
variants kept behind one name. No blanket policy would have been
right: a single "use FMA everywhere" switch would have made syrk
slower and gemm no faster.

## Step 2: the Level-1 assumption race (2026-07-18, runs 29648971889 / 29648968138 / 29648964550)

The implementation-plan table carried three "no hand-SIMD" rows that an
honest audit exposed as asserted, never measured: `swap` ("compiler
auto-vectorizes"), `asum` ("bandwidth-bound; SIMD buys nothing"),
`iamax` ("the branch breaks streaming"). Architect ordered the race.
Harness: `bench/l1-ab.mjs` + `run_l1_ab` — plain scalar loop vs
hand-SIMD (simd128) variant, interleaved, 5 rounds, numeric agreement
cross-checked before timing, verdicts on range separation. ratio > 1
means SIMD is faster.

Three runner draws, three distinct machines (runners 1000000430 /
1000000429 / 1000000428), n = 256 / 512 / 1024:

| op | draw 1 | draw 2 | draw 3 | verdict |
| - | - | - | - | - |
| swap | 1.21× / 1.32× / 1.26× | 1.33× / 1.31× / 1.17× | 1.15× / 1.31× / 1.28× | SIMD WINS, all 9 rows |
| asum | 3.65× / 3.93× / 3.89× | 3.46× / 3.67× / 3.68× | 3.65× / 3.88× / 3.97× | SIMD WINS, all 9 rows |
| iamax | 1.48× / 1.46× / 1.52× | 1.39× / 1.58× / 1.56× | 1.47× / 1.44× / 1.52× | SIMD WINS 8, OVERLAP 1 (draw 1, n=256) |

(Container pre-check agreed in direction for asum 1.80–1.95× and iamax
1.23–1.76×; swap was a wash locally — another case of the container
reading differently from the reference class.)

**All three assumptions are refuted.**

1. **asum is the embarrassment: 3.5–4× left on the table** by the
   "bandwidth-bound" story. The plain loop's serial dependent-add chain
   is latency-bound, nowhere near bandwidth; two independent v128
   accumulators with `f64x2_abs` expose the parallelism.
2. **iamax's branch is beatable**: a branch-free `f64x2_pmax` pass over
   values plus one scalar rescan for the index wins 1.4–1.6×
   everywhere. "The branch breaks streaming" was true of the naive
   shape, not of the operation.
3. **swap wins 1.15–1.33×** — LLVM does auto-vectorize it, but not as
   well as the explicit loop. Margins straddle the 1.3× stability
   threshold, but the verdict is unanimous: 9 of 9 rows range-separated
   SIMD wins across three machines, so the direction is settled even
   where the magnitude is machine-weather.

**Consequence:** the plan table's swap/asum/iamax rows flip to SIMD
streaming loops. Every remaining "no" in the table is now either
measured (`copy` — raced to a dead heat and running at the bandwidth
ceiling) or structural (`rotg` — no arrays to stream). This is the
race-the-foundation rule's third catch this week: every one of the
architect's suspected unraced assumptions was wrong.

## Step 3: Level 1 lands (2026-07-18, runs 29653617403 / 29653615147)

The `blas/` crate's Level 1 shipped (ten f64 functions, one file each;
12 correctness tests per the testing contract in `blas/README.md`).
Getting its first roofline honest exposed **two measurement bugs**:

1. **The layer's first build ran the reductions 6.4× slower than the
   raced variants** — same wasm module, measured side by side. Cause:
   simd128 is NOT in rustc's default wasm32 feature set (the carried
   "default since 1.82" claim was wrong — 1.82 turned on
   bulk-memory/multivalue/etc., not simd128). An unannotated wrapper
   compiled every lane op as an out-of-line function call. Fix: 
   `#[target_feature(enable = "simd128")]` on the whole call chain,
   the pattern kernels/bench already used everywhere — which is WHY
   their raced numbers were always genuine. After the fix the layer
   matches or beats every raced variant.
2. **The step-1 bandwidth-ceiling probe was broken**: `run_ceiling_bw`
   allocated and copied an n×n matrix *inside the timed region*
   (`to_owned` per call) and streamed the destination twice — so the
   step-1 "12.2–17.8 GB/s" ceilings understate the machines and their
   byte accounting was wrong. The v2 probe is a pure single-pass triad
   over persistent state (exactly 3·8·n² bytes, no allocation): the
   same runner class now measures **~32 GB/s triad**. Step-1's FLOPS
   ceilings and all A/B *verdicts* are unaffected (both sides of every
   race paid the same harness), but any absolute GB/s from step 1 is
   superseded.

**Roofline, reference machines** (`bench/l1-roofline.mjs`, n = 2048 so
ops and ceiling both stream from DRAM; scoring ceiling = fastest
same-run stream, because read/write mixes overlap writebacks
differently — the reason STREAM reports four numbers, not one):

| op | draw A GB/s (%) | draw B GB/s (%) |
| - | -: | -: |
| triad reference | 32.3 | 31.6 |
| copy | 32.2 (55%) | 32.1 (67%) |
| swap | 59.0 (100%) | 47.7 (100%) |
| scal | 53.0 (90%) | 42.2 (89%) |
| axpy | 47.7 (81%) | 44.1 (93%) |
| rot | 54.8 (93%) | 47.2 (99%) |
| dot | 30.6 (52%) | 31.8 (67%) |
| nrm2 | 25.8 (44%) | 20.0 (42%) |
| asum | 25.1 (43%) | 19.3 (40%) |
| iamax | 11.5 (19%) | 10.9 (23%) |

Reading, by access class:

- **Read-modify-write streams (swap/scal/axpy/rot): 81–100% of the
  machine's fastest stream on both draws.** Done — no headroom worth
  chasing.
- **Read-dominated (copy/dot): pinned at the triad rate (~32 GB/s,
  95–102% of triad on both draws).** At the read-path ceiling; the
  lower "% of ceiling" against the RMW-inflated scorer is the counting
  artifact, not slack.
- **Dependent-accumulator reductions (nrm2/asum): 60–80% of triad.**
  The 2-register accumulator chain leaves some read bandwidth unused —
  a 4-register variant is a measured-candidate lever, recorded, not
  chased now.
- **iamax: ~70% of triad counting its real traffic** (the score column
  charges only the mandatory single pass, but the value-pass + index
  rescan reads twice). Candidate lever: fuse index tracking into the
  vector pass with lane-index selects. Recorded.

**Cross-target determinism, now structural**: the layer's native build
emulates the simd128 lane layout elementwise (`blas/src/lanes.rs`), so
reductions produce identical bits on every target by construction —
verified on every run: 4/4 probe values bit-identical native ↔ wasm on
the dev container and both runner draws (`run_l1_probe` /
`native l1-bits` / `l1-roofline.mjs`).

- **The rank-1 updates (ger, syr) run at 83–100% of ceiling** — pure

- **gemv/gemv_t sit at ~17 GB/s on both draws** — half the read-path

## Step 6: tuning lever 1 — gemm micro-shapes (2026-07-19, runs 29665765424 / 29665763052)

Two candidates, both **bit-for-bit identical** to the shipped
column-axpy gemm (same per-element rounding sequence — tested across
tile-boundary shapes), raced 4-way against it and faer across sizes on
two runner draws (agreeing within 3% on every cell):

| n | faer | colaxpy | tiled4x4 | col4 | best |
| -: | -: | -: | -: | -: | - |
| 128 | 0.73 | 0.65 | **0.42** | 0.63 | tiled |
| 256 | 5.32 | 4.88 | **3.21** | 4.27 | tiled |
| 384 | 18.45 | 17.04 | **13.21** | 14.38 | tiled |
| 512 | 44.61 | 44.75 | 53.34 | **35.50** | col4 |
| 1024 | 355.93 | 317.97 | 392.76 | **285.42** | col4 |

(draw A ms; draw B within 3%)

- **tiled4x4** (4-row × 4-col tile of C held in 8 SIMD registers
  across the k loop): wins 128–384, up to 1.7× over faer — but its
  k-loop walks A at column stride, and above ~1.5 MB of A that
  strided walk thrashes TLB/prefetch and it LOSES to everything.
- **col4** (four C columns fused per fully sequential pass over A —
  the gemv 2-column lever generalized): 4× less A traffic than
  column-axpy with streaming-friendly access; wins everywhere at
  512+, 1.25× over faer.
- **Shipped**: `gemm` now dispatches on A's byte size (threshold
  1.5 MB, between the measured 384/512 crossover on both draws and
  the container). The plain shape is kept as `gemm_colaxpy` — the
  reference every tuned shape is raced and bit-checked against.
- Net vs faer across the board: gemm now wins at EVERY measured size,
  1.25–1.8×, where the untuned layer traded blows at 512+.

Lesson recorded: register tiling and cache-friendly streaming are in
tension on wasm exactly as on native — the dispatch IS the resolution,
and the same two shapes are the starting candidates for symm/syrk/
trmm/trsm tuning (next levers).

## Step 7: tuning levers 3–4 — the blocked shapes carried through gemv and Level 3 (2026-07-19, container; runner draws pending)

The two gemm shapes were hoisted into shared kernels
(`blas/src/kernels.rs`): **fan-out** (`axpy4` — one source column
streamed once into four destination columns) and **fan-in**
(`axpy4in` — four source columns accumulated in one pass over the
destination). Every remaining Level-3 op plus gemv now ships blocked:

- fan-out, bit-identical to plain: `symm_right` (the gemm col4 shape
  with the symmetric-triangle lookup), `syrk`/`syr2k` (common triangle
  segment vectorized, ≤3-row ragged edge scalar in the same order),
  `trmm_left`/`trsm_left` (four B columns walk the trmv/trsv column
  sweep in lockstep, sharing every A-column read).

## Step 8: tuning lever 5 — fused, 4-column-grouped symv (2026-07-19, container; runner draws pending)

symv's plain shape streamed each stored column twice (axpy for the
stored triangle, dot for the mirrored one). Two moves, landed
together:

1. **Fusion** (`kernels::axpy_dot`): one load of the column element
   serves both the y-update and the dot accumulate. Alone: ~5%
   container — the second stream was already riding L1, so the win is
   loop overhead only.
2. **4-column grouping** (`kernels::axpy_dot4`): symv has no
   cross-column dependencies at all (y is only accumulated into, x/A
   only read), so four stored columns share ONE pass over x and y —
   one y load/store and one x load per element-pair serve four
   columns. Ragged edge and diagonals scalar per column.

## Step 11: the c64 layer lands (2026-07-19, runs 29703517659 / 29703515446)

Andy: "Please implement c64." The first non-mechanical clone — complex
arithmetic has no ready-made lane ops, so the design work was the
product form, not the loop shapes (which ported verbatim).

**Design.** One complex per F64x2 register (`re, im` interleaved —
`src/c64.rs`, repr(C), layout-compatible with num_complex/C99/faer).
Multiplication by a fixed scalar t: precompute `[t.re, t.re]` and
`[−t.im, t.im]`; then `x·vre + swap(x)·vim` gives lane0
`t.re·x.re − t.im·x.im`, lane1 `t.re·x.im + t.im·x.re` — bit-exactly
the canonical scalar C64 order, because the sign folds are exact
((−b)·d ≡ −(b·d), a + (−c) ≡ a − c). Elementwise products (the dots)
use dup0/dup1 + swap with a single-lane sign flip; conjugation IS a
lane-sign choice (neg0 ↔ neg1), which the tests exploit:
`zdotc(x,y) == zdotu(conj x, y)` holds to the BIT, as do
`zgemv_c == zgemv_t(conj A)` and `zgerc == zgeru(conj y)`. Six lane
ops added to lanes.rs (pair/swap/dup0/dup1/neg0/neg1 — exact moves,
emulation trivial); kernels zaxpy4/zaxpy4in/zaxpy_dotc port the
fan-out/fan-in/fused shapes. Six L1 routines are pure delegations to
the tuned d-streams over the 2n-real view (a real
scale/copy/swap/rotation/sum of interleaved complex IS the real op
on 2n elements): zdscal, zcopy, zswap, zdrot, dznrm2 (guards
inherited), dzasum. Divisions (ztrsv/ztrsm/C64::div) use Smith's
algorithm — the dladiv shape, deterministic. Hermitian ops keep
reference DBLE semantics: stored diagonals end exactly real
(+0.0 imag), stored diagonal imag inputs are ignored (tests poison
them to prove it).

**Scope** (26 routines / 31 operations): reference-BLAS z-set minus
what has no consumer — complex-symmetric zsymm/zsyrk/zsyr2k, zrot
(complex-s apply; zrotg already produces its s), all transpose forms,
c32. zgemm ships col4-only: the f64 4×4 register tile is a different
register geometry in complex (one complex per register) — a design
candidate, not a port; recorded as a lever with zhemv's missing
4-column fused grouping.

**Correctness:** 40 new tests, 104 crate-wide, all green. Everything
with a deterministic add order is bit-locked to a same-order scalar
replay (including the grouped trmm/trsm reorder replays and the
herk/her2k real-β + exactly-real-diagonal sequences); reductions are
bounds-tested against component-wise compensated references; zrotg
passes its defining identities at magnitudes 1e±150/±300; solves are
residual-verified with genuinely complex dominant diagonals.

**Determinism:** 24 new probes (5 L1 + 10 L2 + 9 L3, own complex LCG
recipes) — bit-identical native ↔ wasm on the container and BOTH
runner draws. Native modes `l{1,2,3}-bits-z`; scripts take `--c64`.
